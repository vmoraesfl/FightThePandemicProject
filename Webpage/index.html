Fight The Pandemic

Data-driven solutions to predict disease hotspots

CHALLENGE


COVID-19 has infected more than 6 million people and has caused more than 360 thousand deaths worldwide. The COVID-19 outbreak brought into light the huge challenge of combating such an impactful and global pandemic.

Anticipation is key when dealing with such an unique crisis. It has become clear that we all need to better understand its spread patterns, to make informed and accurate health care and public policy decisions.

In such scary and dark times, some questions remain unanswered:
How can technology help prevent and control outbreaks?
How can the world be prepared to better handle the rise of pandemics?

Brazil's Late Response

Delayed response to the COVID-19 pandemic has caused multiple negative effects such as lack of hospital beds, lack of PPEs and of other many medical resources. Not knowing future disease hotspots is one of the main reasons for the unbridled increase of infecteds and deaths numbers.

STRATEGY


Find spread pattern

Based on population density, night time activity and touristic routes, we managed to search for infection patterns.

Predict Hotspots

Based on spread patterns, we can easily predict its future path and hotsposts.

Quick RESPONSES

Provided with such substantial data, we all can rapidly take measures to "flatten the curve".

OUR SOLUTION


NIGHT ACTIVITY & INFECTION MAP

Night time activity data are collected from NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS). Moreover, data for COVID-19 cases are retrieved from SUS, Brazil Government's Health Agency.

OVERLAYING: INCIDENCE OF LIGHT & COVID-19 NOTIFICATION

Areas with a high incidence of light also had a higher occurrence of COVID-19 cases.

Overlaying night time satellite imagery and the map of COVID-19 cases, we were able to distinctly visualize the correlation between population density and infections.

We can easily ascertain the intersection of green points, which represent infection cases, and points of light, indicatives of night activity.

COMPUTING NIGHT LUMINOSITY INFORMATION

We were able to map and process points of light within each county's boundaries. The example shown is from São Paulo. Note that such study can be extended to other multiple territories.

Provided with QGIS open-source software, NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS). and with multiple python data-analysis libraries, we managed to quantify night time luminosity information for each one of the state counties.

FLIGHT ROUTES & DISEASE SPREAD

Early Spread of the Infection data was collected from John Hopkins Coronavirus Resource Center.

Overlaying it and the major flight routes from China, we were able to distinctly visualize the correlation between flight routes and infection. This ilustrates how we used flight data to forecast the disease spread.

DISEASE TRACKING

A data-driven and epidemiological approach to model infectious diseases.

We were able to accurately model the disease spread, with multiple sets of data such as Date of First Infection, Number of Initial Cases, Total Population Numbers, ICU beds per 100k people, Probabilty of going to ICU when infected, etc.
