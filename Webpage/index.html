STRATEGY

Delayed response to the COVID-19 pandemic has caused multiple negative effects such as lack of beds in hospitals, IPE and medical resources.

Unknowing future disease hotspots was one of the main reasons for the unbridled increase of infecteds and deaths numbers.

OUR SOLUTION

NIGHT ACTIVITY & INFECTION MAP

Night time activity data are collected from NASA's Earth Observing System Data and Information System (EOSDIS). Moreover, data for COVID-19 cases are retrieved from SUS, Brazil Government's Health Agency.

OVERLAYING: INCIDENCE OF LIGHT & COVID-19 NOTIFICATION

Areas with a high incidence of light also had a higher occurrence of COVID-19 cases.

Overlaying night time satellite imagery and the map of COVID-19 cases, we were able to distinctly visualize the correlation between population density and infections.

We can easily ascertain the intersection of green points, which represent infection cases, and points of light, indicatives of night activity.

COMPUTING NIGHT LUMINOSITY INFORMATION

We were able to map and process points of light within each county's boundarie.s

Provided with QGIS open-source software and with multiple python data-analysis libraries, we managed to quantify night time luminosity information for each one of the state counties.

The image shows an analysis of the state of São Paulo. Note that such study can be extended to other multiple territories.

DISEASE TRACKING

A data-driven and epidemiological approach to model infectious diseases.

We were able to accurately model the disease spread, with multiple sets of data such as Date of First Infection, Number of Initial Cases, Total Population Numbers, ICU beds per 100k people, Probabilty of going to ICU when infected, etc.
